Example: 62 FT employees (including 4 TRICARE veterans)

Threshold: 62 x 0.95 = 58.9 → round down = 58 offers needed

Employer offers to all 62 (including vets). 4 vets decline, 3 others decline = 55 enrolled.

Result: 62 offered / 62 total = 100% ≥ 95% → PASSES. Enrollment doesn't matter — only the offer.

What if they skip the 4 veterans? 58 / 62 = 93.5% < 95% → FAILS. Penalty: (62 - 30) x $2,900 = $92,800/year.

Critical Correction — EHBs Are NOT Required for MEC:
A common misconception is that employer plans must cover all 10 Essential Health Benefits to qualify as MEC. This is wrong. The 10 EHBs are mandated for individual and small-group marketplace plans only. Large employer (ALE) plans are exempt from the EHB requirement. An ALE's plan qualifies as MEC simply by being a legitimate group health plan that covers basic services including preventive care. Many corporate MEC plans prioritize preventive and wellness coverage without necessarily providing "substantial" hospital-level protection — and they still qualify as MEC.

The MEC Qualification Criteria

  • 1
    It's a Group Health Plan
    The plan must be an employer-sponsored group health plan — not a discount program, health sharing ministry, or informal arrangement. It must be a formal plan that provides health coverage to employees.

    Legal basis: IRC § 5000A(f)(2) defines "eligible employer-sponsored plan" as a group health plan offered by an employer to the employee.
  • 2
    Covers Preventive Care Services
    The plan must cover recommended preventive services. Under ACA Section 2713, non-grandfathered plans must cover USPSTF A/B-rated preventive services, ACIP-recommended immunizations, and HRSA preventive care guidelines with no cost sharing for in-network services.

    What this includes: Annual physicals, immunizations, cancer screenings (mammograms, colonoscopies), well-child visits, contraceptive coverage, blood pressure/cholesterol screenings, and other evidence-based preventive services.

    Legal basis: ACA Section 2713 (PHS Act § 2713), 42 U.S.C. § 300gg-13.
  • 3
    Dependent Coverage to Age 26
    The plan must allow employees to extend coverage to their children up to age 26, regardless of the child's marital status, student status, financial dependency, or whether they live with the employee.

    Legal basis: ACA Section 2714 (PHS Act § 2714), 42 U.S.C. § 300gg-14.
  • 4
    Not Just "Excepted Benefits"
    The plan must provide actual health coverage — not just standalone dental, standalone vision, a health FSA, an EAP (Employee Assistance Program), or a wellness program. These are "excepted benefits" under HIPAA/ACA and do NOT count as MEC by themselves.

    Legal basis: PHS Act § 2791(c), 42 U.S.C. § 300gg-91(c). Excepted benefits are carved out from MEC.

MEC is a Low Bar By Design:
The purpose of MEC is simply to confirm the employer is offering a real health plan — not nothing, and not a sham product. Almost every legitimate employer-sponsored group health plan qualifies. The quality of the plan is measured by Minimum Value (next step), not MEC. Think of MEC as the "existence check" and MV as the "quality check."

Minimum Value: The Two Requirements

RequirementWhat It MeansHow to TestLegal Source
1. Actuarial Value ≥ 60% When you model the plan against a standard population, the plan pays at least 60% of total medical costs. The employee pays the other 40% or less through deductibles, copays, and coinsurance. Use the HHS MV Calculator (an Excel spreadsheet published by CMS). Enter plan design details (deductibles, copays, coinsurance, OOP max). The calculator outputs the actuarial value percentage. Alternatively, get a certified actuary to calculate it. IRC § 36B(c)(2)(C)(ii)
45 CFR § 156.145
ACA § 1302(d)(2)
2. Substantial Hospital & Physician Coverage The plan must include meaningful coverage for inpatient hospital services and physician/outpatient services. Plans that exclude these (e.g., preventive-only "skinny" plans) fail MV even if their AV might technically compute above 60%. Check the plan's SBC. Look for "Hospital stay (facility fee)" and "Doctor visit (primary/specialist)" rows. They must show actual coverage (copay, coinsurance, etc.) — not "Not Covered." IRS Notice 2012-31
Proposed Reg. § 1.36B-6
ACA § 1302(b)(1)

MEC vs MV — A Practical Example:
A "skinny" plan that covers only preventive care and basic doctor visits: MEC = YES (it's a real plan with preventive care). MV = NO (it doesn't cover hospital stays, AV is below 60%). The employer avoids the 4980H(a) penalty (they offered MEC), but faces 4980H(b) risk (if employees get marketplace subsidies, the employer owes $4,350/employee because the plan lacks MV).
How to Check MV in CompliCore
  1. Go to Plans in the sidebar. Select or create the plan.
  2. Enter all plan design details (deductibles, copays, coinsurance, OOP max, HSA/HRA contributions).
  3. Go to MV Calculator. The system calculates actuarial value using the HHS methodology.
  4. Result shows: AV percentage (e.g., 72%), whether it meets the 60% threshold, and whether hospital/physician coverage is included.
  5. If the plan fails MV, consider actuarial certification via the Actuary Marketplace for a formal certification.

Minimum Data Needed

FPL Safe Harbor (easiest): Only needs employee monthly premium. If ≤ $113.20/mo, PASSES automatically. No income data needed.

W-2 Safe Harbor: Needs premium + W-2 wages (or salary).

Rate of Pay: Needs premium + hourly rate.

Shortcut: Set premium ≤ $113.20/mo and every employee passes affordability with zero income data.

Legal Basis for Safe Harbors: All three safe harbors are defined in Treas. Reg. § 54.4980H-5(e). The 9.02% affordability percentage for 2025 is set by Rev. Proc. 2024-35. Originally 9.5% in the ACA statute (IRC § 36B(c)(2)(C)), it's indexed annually.

11 Penalty Calculation — Which Failure Triggers Which Penalty

Exact penalty formulas tied to each compliance failure

4980H(a) — "Sledgehammer"

$2,900/yr

Triggered by:

- Not offering to 95%+ of FT

- Offering a plan that is NOT MEC

Formula: (FT - 30) x $2,900

Example: 62 FT → (62-30) x $2,900 = $92,800/yr

4980H(b) — "Tack Hammer"

$4,350/yr

Triggered by:

- Plan fails Minimum Value

- Coverage is unaffordable

(AND employee gets marketplace subsidy)

Formula: $4,350 x subsidized employees

Example: 5 employees → 5 x $4,350 = $21,750/yr

Fully Compliant

$0

Plan meets MV (60%+)

Coverage is affordable

Employer pays 4980H(a) OR 4980H(b), never both for the same month. The IRS calculates both and applies the lesser amount.

One-Sentence Summary:
An ALE must (1) offer coverage to 95%+ of FT employees including TRICARE veterans, (2) ensure the plan qualifies as MEC (a real group health plan with preventive care — low bar), (3) ensure it provides Minimum Value (60%+ actuarial value with substantial hospital/physician coverage — higher bar), and (4) prove affordability via at least one IRS safe harbor (W-2, Rate of Pay, or FPL) — or face penalties of $2,900/employee for not offering MEC, or $4,350/affected employee for failing MV or affordability.
