2 What Is Minimum Value? — The Two Requirements

Under the ACA, a plan meets the Minimum Value standard if it covers at least 60% of the total allowed cost of benefits expected to be incurred under the plan. This ensures employer-sponsored plans provide meaningful financial protection for employees.

The One Requirement

60%+

The plan must cover at least 60% of total allowed costs for a standard population. The employee pays the remaining 40% or less through deductibles, copays, and coinsurance.

This is the only test. If AV is 60% or above, the plan passes MV.

Penalty for Failing MV

$4,350/yr

Per employee who receives a marketplace premium tax credit subsidy. This is the IRC Section 4980H(b) "tack hammer" penalty.

Starts After MEC

MEC → MV

MV testing only happens after the plan passes MEC. MEC checks if the plan is real. MV checks if the plan is good enough (covers 60%+ of costs).

MEC vs MV: MEC (Minimum Essential Coverage) tests whether you have a real health plan. MV (Minimum Value) tests whether that plan is good enough — specifically, whether it covers at least 60% of expected costs. A plan can pass MEC but fail MV. If it fails MV, employees who go to the marketplace and get subsidized coverage will trigger penalties for the employer.

StepWhatDetails
1. Download & Host Get the official .xlsm file from CMS.gov (4MB, macro-enabled Excel). Host on your server. The HHS MV Calculator is published annually. The platform stores the latest version and maps its cell references to the web form.
2. Build Input Form Build a web form matching the Excel cell inputs exactly. Each field in the web form maps to a specific cell in the Excel workbook. The form validates ranges (e.g., deductible cannot be negative, coinsurance must be 0-100%).
3. Auto-Populate Auto-populate from carrier feeds (JSON/XML) via SFTP or API. Insurance carriers send plan design data electronically. The platform parses the feed and pre-fills the calculator form. The employer can also upload an SBC (PDF) for AI-based extraction.
4. Run Calculation Port the Excel VBA math to a real-time web calculation (3 seconds max). Displays result as: "62.3% MV — PASS" (green) or "58.1% MV — FAIL" (red). Shows breakdown of how each cost-sharing element contributes to the AV percentage.
5. Export PDF Generate IRS-audit-ready PDF: "MV Calculator Results 1/1/2026" PDF includes: plan design inputs, calculation methodology, AV result, PASS/FAIL determination, date, and MV Safe Harbor Notice if applicable.

Document 2: Technical Memorandum (5–10 Pages)

What the Technical Memorandum Contains
  • Methodology: How the actuary calculated AV (claims simulation, custom model, HHS calculator with adjustments)
  • Assumptions: Claims data source (e.g., "2024 MVC claims data + 2% trend"), population modeled, service utilization rates
  • Plan Design Summary: All cost-sharing elements used in the calculation (deductibles, copays, coinsurance, OOP max, Rx tiers)
  • Results: AV percentage with confidence interval, sensitivity analysis showing impact of key assumptions
  • Compliance Statement: ASOP No. 50 compliance declaration, professional credentials

Resubmission Loop: When validation fails, the actuary receives a notification with the specific reason for failure. The actuary corrects the issue in the document and resubmits. The platform re-runs validation automatically. This loop continues until all checks pass or the actuary communicates that the plan cannot meet MV. There is no limit on resubmissions.
What ASOP No. 50 Is: Actuarial Standard of Practice No. 50 is the official rulebook actuaries must follow when testing if employer health plans meet ACA's 60% Minimum Value requirement. What Are MV Safe Harbor Notices?

Purpose: Official IRS "proof of compliance" notice stating your plan was tested using the HHS MV Calculator and achieved 60%+ Minimum Value.

Legal Protection

IRS Notice 2014-69: Employers using calculator + providing notice get safe harbor from MV penalties if audited.

Timing Requirement

90 days: Must notify employees within 90 days of using the calculator method.

When Safe Harbor Applies: The MV Safe Harbor Notice is specifically for employers who used the HHS MV Calculator (Path A). If MV was determined through actuarial certification (Path B), the certification documents themselves serve as the audit-ready proof — a separate safe harbor notice is not needed.

One-Sentence Summary:
A plan meets Minimum Value if it covers at least 60% of total allowed costs (tested via the free HHS MV Calculator or actuarial certification under ASOP 50) and includes substantial coverage for inpatient hospital and physician services — failing MV exposes the employer to a $4,350/year penalty per employee who receives a marketplace subsidy, but employers who use the HHS calculator and issue a Safe Harbor Notice (per IRS Notice 2014-69) are protected from MV penalties if audited.
